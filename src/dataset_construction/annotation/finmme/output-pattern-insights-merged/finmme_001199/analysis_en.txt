The two curves intersect multiple times across the entire period, and their relative positions repeatedly shift across different phases, failing to demonstrate a consistent, single dominant pattern. The chart contains several clear inflection points and localized extreme values, particularly in the low-level range around 2019 and the high-level range around 2022.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The strength of rural wage growth and price growth frequently alternates, showing significant periodic misalignment.

Business Implications:
- The coverage of labor compensation relative to the cost of living exhibits periodic differences.
- The inconsistent pace between income and costs affects the stability of real purchasing power.
- When cost pressures dominate in the later period, residents' disposable income space is compressed.

Risk Focus:
- When CPI ≥7% and wages ≤5%, the real burden significantly increases, potentially leading to consumption contraction.
- When wages are ≤3.5% and persist for two cycles, improvement in rural labor income may stagnate.
