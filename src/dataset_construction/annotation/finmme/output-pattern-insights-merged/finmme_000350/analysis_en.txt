**[Chart Components]**
- Chart Type: Dual Line Chart
- Title: Services less rent of shelter
- Y-axis: Percentage (%), dashed baseline at 0, range approximately -0.5 to 5
- X-axis: Timeline, 2018 to 2024 (Annual scale)
- Legend: Light orange for "3m/3m", Dark red for "6m/6m"

**[Data Relationships]**
- Early 2018: 3m/3m approximately 0.5%, 6m/6m approximately 0.8%.
- End of 2020: 3m/3m lowest point approximately -0.5%; 6m/6m low point approximately 0.3%.
- End of 2022: 3m/3m peak approximately 2.7%; Early 2023: 6m/6m peak approximately 4.8%, the difference between the two is approximately 2.1 percentage points.
- End of 2023: 3m/3m approximately 0.8%; 6m/6m approximately 1.3%.
- Early 2024: 3m/3m approximately 1.2%; 6m/6m approximately 2.4%.
- 6m/6m peak-to-trough difference approximately 4.5 percentage points; 3m/3m peak-to-trough difference approximately 3.2 percentage points.

**[Pattern Characteristics]**
- From 2018 to 2020, the two curves fluctuated slightly around lower levels, with 3m/3m showing an extreme low point at the end of 2020, clearly crossing below the zero axis.
- Starting in 2021, both curves generally trended upward, forming a single major peak in 2022-2023, where the 6m/6m peak was significantly higher than the 3m/3m peak, followed by a synchronized decline.
- Since the end of 2023, both curves have again risen in the same direction within the lower range. 6m/6m consistently remains above 3m/3m, and the overall structure is continuous without sudden spikes.

**[Core Insights]**
Core Conclusion: The cyclical pressure on services prices (excluding rent) significantly amplified and then retreated, but has recently intensified again.

Business Implications:
- The high points in 2022-2023 indicate a significant phase of price elevation for this component.
- 6m/6m consistently being higher than 3m/3m reflects a more pronounced cumulative increase over the medium term.
- The renewed strengthening after the end of 2023 may undermine the previous trend of moderation.

Risk Focus:
- If 6m/6m approaches 4.8% again, it could lead to pressure on overall services inflation.
- If 3m/3m returns above 2.7%, short-term upward price momentum may strengthen again.
