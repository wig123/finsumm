[Pattern Characteristics]
- The structure across all periods is consistently dominated by the 10k-25k and 25k-50k segments, while the <10k segment remains in a low proportion range.
- The 10k-25k segment was relatively high during the middle periods, then gradually declined, while the 25k-50k segment continuously rose from a lower level, becoming the highest proportion segment in the final period.
- The >50k segment slowly increased throughout the entire period, slightly retreating after reaching a relative high point in FY23. The overall structure shifted from small-to-medium ticket sizes towards medium-to-large ticket sizes, with no significant single-period abrupt changes.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The structure of new originations is concentrating towards higher ticket sizes.

Business Implications:
- The proportion of small amounts is decreasing, indicating an increase in the average ticket size per customer.
- The mid-range 25k-50k segment has become the primary source of incremental volume.
- The weight of large amounts (>50k) has increased, making the portfolio more reliant on a few large transactions.

Risk Focus:
- If the >50k proportion consistently exceeds 23%, a single large default will amplify losses.
- If the <10k proportion remains below 4% for an extended period, it is detrimental to diversification and coverage of lower-tier customer segments.
