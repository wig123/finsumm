[Pattern Characteristics]
Overall, values repeatedly cross between the negative and positive ranges, showing alternating multi-stage uptrends and pullbacks. In the early 1990s, values were mainly concentrated near zero, slightly negative. After 1998, they entered a high positive range plateau lasting several years, forming a distinct peak around 2002–2004. Although values approached or reached high positive levels multiple times thereafter, they did not exceed the previous peak. After a brief pullback around 2012, they returned to the medium-to-high positive range.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: USD valuation has undergone multiple repricings over the long term, remaining generally high in recent years.

Business Implications:
- Valuation levels show significant volatility over the long term, lacking a single central tendency.
- A historically high overvaluation phase occurred in the early 2000s.
- Valuation has been maintained in a relatively high range since 2015–2016.

Risk Concerns:
- When exceeding approximately 15%, it may trigger pressure for a more significant correction.
- A rapid drop from around positive 10% to below zero could trigger a substantial revaluation of finance and asset pricing.
