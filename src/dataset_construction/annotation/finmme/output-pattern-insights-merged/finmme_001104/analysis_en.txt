[Core Insights]

Core Conclusion: The portion held by the Federal Reserve (Fed) significantly extends the overall duration, while a high proportion of short-term bills suppresses the market-side duration.

Business Implications:
- Current market-side duration is lower than the overall duration, resulting in limited interest rate sensitivity.
- The Fed's asset portfolio has a longer duration, making it more sensitive to changes in long-term interest rates.
- After excluding short-term bills, market and overall duration rise significantly, indicating a high concentration of maturities.

Risk Focus:
- If the Market WAM drops below 4 years, price volatility during rising interest rates will be limited, but refinancing pressure will increase.
- If SOMA WAM remains above 10 years, an upward shift in long-term interest rates will amplify asset valuation volatility.
