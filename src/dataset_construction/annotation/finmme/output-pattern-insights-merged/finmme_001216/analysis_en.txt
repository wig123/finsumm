[Pattern Characteristics]
- The pink bars generally show a stepped increase, reaching a clear high point in 1Q23 in the middle-to-later period, followed by a slight decline before stabilizing in a higher range.
- The gray line oscillates frequently throughout the period, showing multiple sharp extreme points, particularly forming distinct inverse extremes (one high, one low) around 3Q16 and 1Q17.
- In recent quarters, while the high level of inventory value has continued, the YoY growth rate rapidly retreated from its previous extremely high level to near zero before slightly rebounding.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Inventory scale has significantly expanded, but the growth rate has clearly slowed down.

Business Implications:
- Capital tied up in inventory has increased significantly, requiring more stringent capital allocation.
- The alignment between replenishment and sales pace is becoming more critical.
- Future inventory efficiency will directly impact earnings flexibility.

Risk Focus:
- When inventory approaches $1,750 million USD and YoY exceeds 70%, if demand falls short of expectations, it could lead to significant markdowns and write-downs.
- When YoY drops near 0% and inventory remains above $1,500 million USD, slower turnover may significantly compress gross margin and cash flow.
