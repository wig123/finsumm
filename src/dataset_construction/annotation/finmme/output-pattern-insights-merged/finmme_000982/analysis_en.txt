[Pattern Characteristics]
The grey bars gradually increased from 2010 to 2019, with multiple minor pullbacks occurring intermittently. They rose significantly in 2022 and moved further upward in 2023. The YoY growth rate labels corresponding to the bars repeatedly switch above and below the zero axis. A single extremely high value appeared in 2022, which pulled back in 2023 but remained in a high range. The red line generally maintained minor fluctuations close to a horizontal level from 2010 to 2019. It briefly fell below the zero axis in 2020, then rapidly recovered and reached the highest point of the entire period in 2023, forming a distinct low-point to high-point structure.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Profit recovery driven by net interest income was concentrated and released after 2022.

Business Implications:
- Profitability is highly dependent on interest income, indicating a non-diversified structure.
- Profit recovery after 2020 mainly stemmed from improved net interest margin.
- Recent profitability levels are significantly higher than the historical normal range.

Risk Concerns:
- If YoY net interest income falls below 0% again, net income may once again approach zero or even turn negative.
- If YoY net interest income rapidly declines from 29% to single digits, profitability levels may significantly retreat to the 2010-2019 range.
