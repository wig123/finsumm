[Pattern Characteristics]
- The total penetration rate continuously rose from the starting point, showed a single prominent high point at the end of 2022, and subsequently stabilized within a plateau range slightly below the peak, accompanied by significant monthly fluctuations.
- The combined proportion of BEV and PHEV approached or slightly exceeded that of HEV in the middle-to-late period. The upper HEV segment remained generally stable, and the structural balance among the three components became more pronounced in the later period.
- The chart shows multiple instances of short-term spikes and pullbacks, but no sustained one-sided trend was formed. Local high points are mostly concentrated at year-end or in adjacent months.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Electrification penetration has entered a high-level plateau phase, with a more balanced structure.

Business Implications:
- The overall level of electrification has significantly improved compared to the starting point.
- HEV is no longer absolutely dominant; the importance of BEV/PHEV is rising.
- The pace within the year is uneven, making the management of short-term timing more critical.

Risk Focus:
- If total penetration remains below 40% for an extended period, it may weaken investment enthusiasm for electrification.
- If the proportion of BEV+PHEV falls below 24%, it may affect related capacity and supporting infrastructure planning.
