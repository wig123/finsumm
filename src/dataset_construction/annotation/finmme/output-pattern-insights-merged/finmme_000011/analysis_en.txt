[Data Relationships]
- The state with the highest tax rate is Louisiana, with a tax rate around 12%
- Oregon has the lowest tax rate, nearly 0%
- The tax rates for most states are between 4% and 8%
- In many states, the "Max local" is significantly higher than the "Base" tax rate
- The overall trend shows that higher local tax rates significantly increase the total tax rate in some states

[Pattern Characteristics]
The chart is sorted by total tax rate from low to high. Bar lengths in the middle segment are similar, while only a few states at the top form a long tail. Each state's bar is composed of the Base and Max local segments, showing significant differences in proportion; some are almost entirely the base segment, while there are also instances where only the local segment is visible. Extreme values are clear: Louisiana is at the very top, and Oregon is close to zero.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Local surcharges dominate the differences in tax burden among states

Business Implications:
- Cross-state pricing and profit calculation require separate listing of local components
- 4%–8% can serve as the median reference band
- High-ranking states require stronger cost transfer arrangements

Risk Concerns:
- Total Tax Rate ≥ 10%: If prices are not adjusted, profit will be passively compressed
- Total Tax Rate > 8% still estimated using 4%–8%: Understated budget, profit deviation
