[Pattern Characteristics]
- Both series were in a relatively high range around 1950–1970, after which they showed a continuous overall downward trend. The decline in the union membership proportion was steeper and greater in magnitude.
- During the middle and later periods, the wage proportion showed several brief rebounds and local peaks during its decline, while the trend of the union membership proportion was relatively smoother.
- Around the mid-1970s, the downward slope of both curves significantly increased compared to the previous period, forming a noticeable inflection point characteristic. Subsequently, the gap between the union membership proportion and the wage proportion gradually widened.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: As the proportion of union membership significantly declines, the proportion of wages in total income weakens synchronously.

Business Implications:
- The bargaining power of labor in overall income distribution continues to diminish.
- The ability of labor compensation to support the macro income structure is decreasing.
- The labor income structure is increasingly reliant on non-unionized labor market mechanisms.

Risk Focus:
- If the wage proportion of GDI remains close to or falls below 42% for an extended period, it may weaken the support of household income for the economy.
- If the union membership proportion further approaches or falls below 8%, the coordination costs for labor-management conflicts may noticeably increase.
