[Pattern Characteristics]
The four series start from the same level near the origin, subsequently diverging during 2021-2022, with the Red series showing significantly greater magnitude and duration of increase than the other three. All series rapidly retreated from high levels near the end of 2022 and remained generally below zero throughout 2023, but the relative ranking at the end remains stable: Black lowest, followed by Light Orange, Light Gray, and Red highest. The Red series experienced a brief rebound (retracement) in mid-2023, while the other three moved downward more smoothly.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Upstream segments exhibit greater volatility, while downstream profit margins face more prolonged pressure.

Business Implications:
- Upstream pricing power is stronger, and cyclicality is more pronounced.
- Downstream module and cell profitability is more squeezed and recovery is lagged.
- Profit distribution across the entire chain shows a pattern of upstream concentration and downstream weakness.

Risk Concerns:
- If the upstream series approaches 200% or higher again, the downstream may struggle to pass on costs synchronously.
- If the cell or module series consistently remains below -50%, downstream companies' cash flow and capacity utilization may face significant pressure.
