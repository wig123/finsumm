[Pattern Characteristics]
The two series (bars and triangles) generally show a synchronized, gradually ascending step structure across the fiscal years, with greater increases observed in the later period. Changes between years are continuous, with no significant decline, except for the FY21–FY22 period where the triangle values (proportion) remained largely flat. Both the bar height and the triangle height for FY23 and FY25F are significantly higher than in previous years, forming two relatively prominent peaks, with FY25F being the most concentrated in the upper range.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The scale and proportion of green power are increasing simultaneously, accelerating structural adjustment.

Business Implications:
- The weighting of green power in the overall power structure continues to rise.
- Capacity expansion and structural optimization show a trend of synergistic advancement.
- Resource and capital allocation towards green power will be more concentrated over the next two years.

Risk Concerns:
- If FY25F capacity is below 500MW, the green power proportion may struggle to stabilize above 60%.
- If the proportion remains near 50% for an extended period, the return on existing expansion investment may be weakened.
