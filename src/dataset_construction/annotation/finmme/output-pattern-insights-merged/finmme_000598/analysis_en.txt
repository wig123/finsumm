[Pattern Characteristics]
The two indices generally tracked closely from Jan-14 to around Jan-18, fluctuating slightly above and below the baseline. After Jan-18, the light line visibly weakened first and broke below its previous range, while the dark line remained relatively stable before gradually rising. Starting from Jan-21, both lines moved upward synchronously but with different slopes. The dark line was significantly higher than the light line around 2023, and the gap between the two in Jan-24 is at a relatively high level, though slightly below the peak.

[Core Insights]

Core Conclusion: Prices of tariff-impacted goods exhibit stronger relative upward momentum and stickiness.

Business Implications:
- Price pressure is more pronounced for tariff-impacted goods relative to non-tariff goods.
- Tariff-impacted goods contribute more significantly to overall goods inflation.
- During periods of price decline, tariff-impacted goods show weaker adjustment elasticity.

Risk Focus:
- When the Tariff-impacted index > 120 and the gap with the other index is ≥ 8, this may lead to concentrated structural price pressure.
- If the Overall goods ex-food index again approaches or falls below 95, while Tariff-impacted remains > 115, this could intensify the divergence in the price system, leading to a crowding-out effect on demand for related categories.
