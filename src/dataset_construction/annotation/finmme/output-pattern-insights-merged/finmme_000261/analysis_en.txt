[Pattern Characteristics]
In the left chart, the Net Position curve shows multiple distinct phases of upward and downward movement around the zero line. The amplitude of these swings is large, with an extreme unidirectional extension appearing in the recent forecast segment. The stacked area shows significant changes in the proportion of each component across different phases, but components are mostly concentrated in the same direction, with counter-directional components being relatively small. In the right chart, the four interest rate-related values for each economy form vertical ranges of varying heights. Some groups show large internal discrepancies, while others are relatively tight. The forecast policy rate or neutral rate for individual economies significantly deviates from the remaining values.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The direction of monetary policy adjustments among major economies is highly synchronized, and the overall stance is restrictive.

Business Implications:
- The impact of the global interest rate environment on national financial conditions is becoming more uniform.
- Policy rates in most economies remain above the estimated neutral level.
- The future pace of easing is more likely to be gradual rather than involving sharp adjustments.

Risk Focus:
- Risk 1: If the net position approaches the lower limit of the left chart, around ‑12, it could trigger amplified asset price volatility resulting from global easing.
- Risk 2: If a country's end-2024 policy rate remains persistently more than approximately 2 percentage points above its neutral rate, it could intensify local growth and financing cost pressures.
