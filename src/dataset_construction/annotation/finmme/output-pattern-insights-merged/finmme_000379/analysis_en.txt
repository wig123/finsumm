- Countries with 0% change are concentrated in the middle, forming a consistent, flat segment, while the distribution gradually widens towards the positive and negative directions on both sides.
- The right end is characterized by highly prominent extreme positive values formed by a few countries, while the left end consists of a cluster of relatively close negative values across multiple countries. Overall, there are no sudden, discontinuous jumps.

[Core Insights]

Core Conclusion: The Gini index in most countries showed an upward trend between 2000 and 2022, with a few countries experiencing a decline or remaining largely unchanged.

Business Implications:
- Generally reflects a global trend towards higher concentration in income distribution.
- Creates greater pressure on social stability and consumption structures in countries with high increases.
- Countries with zero change may exhibit structural rigidity, requiring external variables to drive adjustment.

Risk Focus:
- Economies with increases exceeding 20% may exacerbate social conflict if not accompanied by corresponding redistribution mechanisms.
- Countries in the −5% to −7% range, if accompanied by slowing growth, may experience new forms of class stratification.
