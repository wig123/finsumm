  - Bottom Right: BBCA - Monthly profit IDRbn
- Axes:
  - Y-axis: Unit is IDRbn (Indonesian Rupiah billion)

- Legend:
  - Red bar chart represents "Monthly profit IDRbn"

[Data Relationships]

- **BBNI**
  - Monthly profit steadily increased throughout the period, growing from approximately 2,500 IDRbn to about 3,500 IDRbn.
  - The 12-Month Moving Average (12MMA) also shows a continuous upward trend.

- **BMRI**
  - Initial profit stabilized between approximately 3,500 IDRbn and 4,000 IDRbn.
  - Starting in 2021, profit increased significantly, reaching approximately 6,500 IDRbn by 2024.
  - The 12MMA shows a similar upward trend, indicating robust overall profit growth.

- **BBRI**
  - Monthly profit grew from approximately 5,500 IDRbn to about 10,500 IDRbn, a significant increase.
  - The 12MMA line shows a steady rise, increasing from 6,000 IDRbn to nearly 10,000 IDRbn.

- **BBCA**
  - Initial profit was approximately 3,000 IDRbn, gradually increasing to about 6,000 IDRbn in 2024.
  - The 12MMA curve shows an upward trend, consistent with the growth in monthly profit.

- **Overall Trend**
  - The monthly profits and 12-Month Moving Averages for all banks show an upward trend, indicating stable profit growth, especially after 2021.
  - Profit growth for BBRI and BMRI is particularly notable, showing a higher growth rate compared to other banks.

[Pattern Characteristics]
- In all four charts, the bars fluctuate slightly around the 12MMA, and the lines are smooth and continuous; in terms of magnitude, BBRI and BMRI are higher, followed by BBCA, and BBNI is the lowest.
- After 2021, the slope for BMRI and BBRI is steeper; BBCA showed consecutive months above the average at the end of 2023; BBNI had one month significantly below the average near the end of the period.
- There are no long-term discontinuities; local peaks above the average alternate with pullbacks, and the overall structure is predominantly continuous.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Profit hierarchy is clear, BBRI/BMRI dominate, followed by BBCA.

Business Implications:
- Clear stratification of profitability
- Allocation can focus on high-profit entities
- BBCA has a greater short-term impact on the average

Risk Concerns:
- BMRI monthly profit < 5,000 IDRbn would depress the 12MMA, weakening its relative advantage.
- BBCA monthly profit < 5,500 IDRbn, making the recent improvement in the average difficult to sustain.
