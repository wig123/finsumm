[Pattern Characteristics]
- Total volume gradually increased from the low level at the start of the period, reaching a clear peak at the end of 2022, and subsequently fluctuating within a higher range but not returning to earlier levels.
- Structurally, BEV has long been the sole dominant factor. ICE significantly increased its volume after mid-2022. Hybrid and PHEV only began to form a visible proportion in 2023.
- A prominent peak appeared at the end of 2022, followed by a clear decline during mid-2023, and subsequently returned to a level close to the previous peak.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Exports to Western Europe are highly dependent on electric vehicle models, with the structure gradually diversifying.

Business Implications:
- The export scale is generally in an expansion phase.
- Revenue and bargaining power are more dependent on the performance of New Energy Vehicles (NEVs).
- The growth of ICE and plug-in hybrid categories enhances portfolio flexibility.

Risk Concerns:
- If average monthly exports fall below 40,000 units, the scale effect will be significantly weakened.
- If the BEV proportion falls below 50% of the total volume, it may weaken the differentiated positioning in the target market.
