The two lines generally oscillate repeatedly around the range between 18 and 20, and the gray banded background essentially covers this range. [Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: USDZAR repeatedly deviates from and reverts to the BEER+ valuation band.

Business Implications:
- Phased misalignment often occurs in exchange rate relative valuation.
- Misalignment phases are accompanied by differences in position performance.
- The valuation band exerts a certain constraint on price.

Risk Focus:
- When USDZAR is above 20 and far from the upper edge of the gray band, it may lead to the appearance of sustained "loss" segments.
- When USDZAR is below 18 and close to the lower edge of the gray band, a lack of rapid reversion may increase drawdown pressure.
