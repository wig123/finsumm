[Pattern Characteristics]
The two curves generally show multiple large fluctuations, with clear low point ranges appearing between 2017–2021. The credit curve rapidly increased after forming a deep "V" shape near 2018 and 2021, while the deposit curve first showed a sharp peak near 2017, followed by relatively smoother volatility around the central axis. There are multiple periods where the two are close or overlap, and also recent periods where credit is significantly higher than deposits.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The pace of recent credit expansion is significantly faster than the deposit growth rate.

Business Implications:
- The support provided by funding sources for credit extension is weakening.
- The dependence of asset side scale on the liability side is increasing.
- Pressure on liquidity and funding cost management is intensifying.

Risk Focus:
- If credit growth persistently remains ≥16% and deposits ≤13%, it may lead to increased reliance on interbank financing and pressure on net interest margins (NIM).
- If the difference between the two is ≥5 percentage points and persists for more than one year, it may trigger liquidity mismatch and regulatory constraint pressure.
