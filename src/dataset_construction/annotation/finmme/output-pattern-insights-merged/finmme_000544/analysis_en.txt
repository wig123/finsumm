[Pattern Characteristics]
- The bar heights for different companies vary significantly across all periods. The internal dispersion is greatest in the FY20-22 group, followed by 9MFY24, with other periods showing relative convergence.
- BAF and CIFC consistently occupy the higher positions within their respective groups across all periods, while MMFS generally remains in the lower range, registering the only negative value in FY20-22.
- From FY20-22 to FY23, and then to 9MFY24, the direction of change in bar height between adjacent periods is inconsistent for most companies. A few companies (such as SHFL, MMFS) experienced a decline after FY23.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Differentiation intensified during the pandemic period, after which the leaders established an absolute lead.

Business Implications:
- During the pandemic, the pace of expansion for certain institutions showed a clear divergence from peers.
- In the post-pandemic phase, leading players have gained an advantage in book value accumulation.
- Lagging institutions are more dependent on industry prosperity for value creation.

Risk Focus:
- If MMFS remains below 5% long-term or repeats the negative value seen in FY20-22, it could weaken capital buffers.
- If BAF or CIFC consistently exceeds 35%, the widening gap with peers may trigger competitive pressure.
