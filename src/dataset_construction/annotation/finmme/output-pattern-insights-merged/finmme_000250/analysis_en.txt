[Pattern Characteristics]
Over time, the proportion of traditional gasoline and diesel components in the total composition continues to contract, while electric-related drivetrains (BEV, Plug-in) gradually become the dominant type. Compositional differences exist between the scenarios (S1/S2/S3/LIFE) in 2040 and 2050, but all show a concentrated structure dominated by a single powertrain type. Fuel cells and gaseous ICE consistently maintain only a marginal proportion. The overall change is continuous, without obvious sudden jumps.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The fleet powertrain type is highly concentrated towards electrification, and the proportion of internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles is significantly compressed.

Business Implications:
- The proportion of traditional fuel-related business declines significantly.
- OEMs and the supply chain must focus their strategy on electric products.
- The market space for fuel cell and gaseous technologies is limited across multiple scenarios.

Risk Focus:
- If Diesel + Gasoline falls below 40% in 2030, ICE vehicle assets may experience declining utilization and depreciation.
- If the electric proportion exceeds 80% in 2050, insufficient charging infrastructure and supporting facilities will constrain vehicle usage and sales conversion.
