- The three inventory categories consistently form a stable three-tier structure across the entire period, but the relative proportions of Raw Materials and Finished Goods show a clear inverse relationship (trade-off) across different stages.
- Raw Materials were at a moderate level early on, then remained in a relatively high range during the middle-to-later stages; WIP generally stayed within a narrow range with minor fluctuations; Finished Goods relatively contracted in the middle stage and rose again in the later stage.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The inventory structure shifted from being dominated by Finished Goods to being dominated by Raw Materials.

Business Implications:
- The middle-to-later period relies more on upstream input rather than downstream backlog.
- The overall inventory turnover pace may be more driven by production planning.
- The stable WIP proportion indicates that the production process is relatively controllable.

Risk Concerns:
- When the Raw Materials proportion approaches 66%, weakened demand will amplify losses due to price fluctuations.
- If the Finished Goods proportion again approaches 50%, sluggish sales will increase inventory capital occupation.
