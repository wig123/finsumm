[Pattern Characteristics]
Overall, the total height of the bars across the years is similar, but the internal composition shows significant redistribution, gradually transitioning from a single dominant component to a structure where multiple components have similar proportions. Tractors long held absolute dominance in the early stages, followed by a continuous narrowing of its proportion, while the UV proportion continuously rose and approached or even surpassed Tractors in the later period. LCV and Spares showed relatively moderate changes across periods. M HCV consistently remained in a narrow range close to the zero axis and slightly negative, without sharp fluctuations.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The product structure is shifting from high concentration in Tractors to a diversified composition led by UV.

Business Implications:
- Reduced dependence on Tractors, leading to more diversified revenue streams.
- The rising proportion of UV will reshape the overall profitability structure.
- Stability in LCV and Spares provides a certain buffer.

Risk Focus:
- If the Tractors proportion continues to fall below 30%, the decline of a single high-margin source may compress profits.
- If the UV proportion exceeds 45%, sensitivity to sales fluctuations in a single category will significantly increase.
