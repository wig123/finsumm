- Contribution of each layer in 2028 from largest to smallest: Top 5 U.S. Cloud SPs > Rest of Cloud > Large Enterprises > Rest of Enterprise ≈ Telco SPs > Top 3 Chinese Cloud SPs.
- The corresponding value for Top 5 U.S. Cloud SPs in 2028 is the highest among all layers, located in the range above $20B; Top 3 Chinese Cloud SPs are below $5B.

[Pattern Characteristics]
- The overall area gradually expands over time, and clear boundaries are consistently maintained between the sub-layers, with no significant contraction or discontinuity.
- The proportion of the Top 5 U.S. Cloud SPs layer in the total is continuously increasing, becoming the primary source of area, while the area of other tiers expands relatively moderately.
- The area represented by Top 3 Chinese Cloud SPs and Telco SPs is thin compared to all layers, and the change over the timeline is relatively smooth, without prominent inflection points or sudden jumps.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The revenue structure, driven by leading U.S. cloud vendors in the data center market, is further strengthened.

Business Implications:
- Revenue is highly dependent on a few hyperscale cloud customers.
- Cloud-related customers have a greater impact on overall revenue elasticity.
- Enterprise and telecom customers provide a relatively secondary contribution to the structure.

Risk Concerns:
- If revenue from Top 5 U.S. Cloud SPs falls below $20B, the overall scale may be significantly lower than expected.
- If Top 3 Chinese Cloud SPs remain below $5B long-term, it will limit regional and customer structure diversification.
