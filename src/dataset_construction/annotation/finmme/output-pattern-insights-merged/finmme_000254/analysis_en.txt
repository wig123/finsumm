[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Shareholder return is significantly amplified and sustained over a longer period after excluding cash and ICDs.

Business Implications:
- Indicates that the return level of core operating assets is significantly higher than the overall return.
- The overall RoE is more influenced by the allocation strategy of low-yield assets.
- Reflects a higher contribution of core business to shareholder value.

Risk Concerns:
- If RoE remains below 10% for the long term, the overall shareholder return may struggle to support the current capital deployment.
- If the gap between RoE and the ex-cash metric consistently exceeds 20 percentage points, it may increase pressure for the disposal and reallocation of inefficient assets.
