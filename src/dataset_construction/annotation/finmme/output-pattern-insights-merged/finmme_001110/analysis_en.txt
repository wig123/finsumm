Overall, an initial benchmark value is decomposed downwards into multiple negative items, resulting in a continuous decrease, followed by the formation of a lower platform value in the middle. Structurally, it is divided into three segments: "US Manufacturing Cost → China Manufacturing Cost → Landed Cost." The first segment involves multiple dispersed contributions, while the second segment involves a concentration of a few items driving the value up, with no obvious sudden jumps.

**[Core Insights]**
Core Conclusion: After shifting production to China, the total cost, even including landed costs, remains significantly lower than US domestic manufacturing.

Business Implications:
- Direct production costs in China are significantly lower than US domestic manufacturing levels.
- Cross-border transportation and associated costs partially erode the overall savings.
- Cost savings primarily stem from structural differences such as payroll and fixed facilities.

Risk Focus:
- If logistics-related costs increase by another 10 points compared to the current value, the overall savings margin will narrow significantly.
- If the China manufacturing cost index rises from 48 to 63, the current cost difference will be completely offset.
