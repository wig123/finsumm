- Overall, there is no regular cyclical fluctuation; instead, the pattern is dominated by long-term gradual evolution, interspersed with a few short-term variations of significant magnitude.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The development of various transport modes is highly unbalanced, and their sensitivity to sudden shocks differs significantly.

Business Implications:
- Aviation exhibits higher expansiveness and stronger external dependence.
- Road and Maritime assume similar roles as fundamental transport capacity over the long term.
- Rail is relatively weakened, and its overall allocation weight has significantly decreased.

Risk Focus:
- When the Aviation index falls below 100 (such as in 2020), related capacity and revenue volatility may be severely amplified.
- The Rail index remaining below 50 long-term and continuing to decline may lead to further weakening of network coverage and service capability.
