[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The funding source structure is shifting from diversified dispersion toward concentration in Banks and Other institutions.

Business Implications:
- Increased reliance on the banking sector, leading to changes in bargaining power.
- Reduced influence of Non-Banking Financial Institutions (NBFIs) within the overall structure.
- Following the exit of AIFIs, the replacement force primarily comes from other unclassified institutions.

Risk Concerns:
- If the Banks proportion consistently exceeds 55, volatility from a single source may amplify overall financing uncertainty.
- If the Mutual funds proportion falls below 14 and continues to decline, it may weaken the flexibility of market-based funding sources.
