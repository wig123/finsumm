[Pattern Characteristics]
- In the top-left chart, local and foreign weightings fluctuate narrowly within the range, generally running parallel to the price curve but not perfectly synchronized. The MSCI and JCI weighting benchmarks remain largely flat.
- In the top-right chart, foreign ownership proportion is consistently higher than local ownership. Retail holdings gradually increased starting from the middle period. The stock price fluctuated around a central pivot point in the latter half of the period.
- In the bottom-left chart, Local IS holds a clearly dominant position in the middle-to-late sample period, while MF and PF remain at low and relatively stable levels.
- In the bottom-right chart, Foreign PF is consistently the primary holder and further increased its share in the later period. MF maintained a moderate level, and IS remained at an extremely low proportion long-term.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The funding structure is dominated by institutional investors, with a bias towards long-term foreign allocation.

Business Implications:
- The difference in local and foreign allocation reflects stratification in the holder structure.
- The dominance of Foreign PF increases the stock price's reliance on long-term capital.
- The rising proportion of retail holdings increases the activity of trading accounts.

Risk Concerns:
- If the Foreign PF proportion significantly declines from 7.4%, it could lead to downward pressure on the stock price and increased turnover.
- If the retail proportion continues to rise rapidly from 5.3%, volatility may intensify and magnify short-term drawdown risk.
