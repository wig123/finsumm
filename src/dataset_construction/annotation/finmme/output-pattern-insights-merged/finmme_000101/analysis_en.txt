[Data Relationships]
- Key values and changes:
  - PX spread initially (2019) remained above 500, then decreased after 2020, fluctuating generally between 300 and 400, and rose again close to 400 at the end of 2023.
  - PTA spread is generally lower than the PX spread, fluctuating consistently between 0 and 300.
  - MEG spread was initially low, hovering around 100, rebounded slightly after bottoming out in 2020, but remained between 0 and 200.

- Extreme Value Comparison:
  - PX spread reached its highest point above 600 in early 2019.
  - MEG spread fell to its lowest point, nearly -200, in mid-2020.

- Important Trends:
  - The overall trend of the PX spread is a decline followed by a slight recovery.
  - PTA spread is relatively stable, showing a slight increase.
  - MEG spread first declined and then recovered, but the magnitude of the increase was limited.

[Pattern Characteristics]
- The three series show a clear hierarchy: the red line is highest, the black line is central, and the gray line is lowest, fluctuating within their respective ranges.
- The distance between the three narrowed around 2020, then widened again; a brief spike occurred in mid-2022, accompanied by a significant dip in the gray line.
- In the closing phase, the red line maintains a relatively high platform, the black line is in the middle range, and the gray line, although having moved up slightly, remains in the bottom range.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Upstream spread dominates, midstream is secondary, and downstream earnings elasticity is weak.

Business Implications:
- Reflects the skewing of profit distribution toward the upstream sector.
- PTA is highly susceptible to upstream influence, resulting in weak bargaining power.
- MEG has low profit elasticity, and its potential for improvement is limited.

Risk Focus:
- MEG spread ≤ 0 or approaching -200 indicates potential sustained losses.
- When PX spread ≥ 500, cost pressure on PTA/MEG increases, and profit margins are compressed.
