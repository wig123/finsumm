The PCR line gradually increased after stabilizing at the same level in the early period, reaching a clear peak in FY23, followed by a slight decline but remaining at a relatively high level. There are no sharp fluctuations among the three metrics; the pace of change is relatively smooth, with only a significant high point for PCR forming around FY23.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Non-performing asset ratios are declining while provision coverage remains within a high range.

Business Implications:
- The extent of non-performing asset exposure is gradually mitigating.
- Provisioning for non-performing assets is more adequate.
- The alignment between asset quality and impairment provisioning has improved.

Risk Concerns:
- If GNPA approaches 6% or higher again, it may compress profit margins.
- If PCR falls back near 35%, it may weaken the capacity to buffer against subsequent increases in non-performing assets.
