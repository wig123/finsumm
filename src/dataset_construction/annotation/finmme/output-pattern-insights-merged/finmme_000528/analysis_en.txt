- Type: Informational table, without axes or legends.
- Column headers: Metric no., Driver of nature change, Indicator, Metric, Connection to GBF targets.

[Data Relationships]
- C2.3: Indicator is Plastic pollution; measurement is the total weight of the plastic footprint (tons), broken down by raw material composition.
- C2.3 provides 4 percentage attributes for plastic packaging: Reusable, Compostable, Technically recyclable, Actually and scalably recyclable (4 categories in total).
- C2.4: Indicator is Non‑GHG air pollutants; measurement is the weight of non-greenhouse gas air pollutants (tons), categorized by type.
- C2.4 types total 5 categories: Particulate matter (PM2.5/PM10), Nitrogen oxides (NO2, NO, NO3), Volatile organic compounds (VOC or NMVOC), Sulfur oxides (SO2, SO, SO3, SOx), Ammonia (NH3).
- The GBF connection for both metrics is Target 7 and Target 11 (B.1).

[Pattern Characteristics]
The table presents two measurements in parallel: one focusing on the quantity and attributes of plastic, and the other focusing on the types of non-GHG air pollutants. Each measurement is further subdivided into multiple subcategories or percentage breakdowns, structurally presenting a hierarchy of "one main metric - multiple sub-items." Both measurements point to the same combination of targets in the GBF target column, demonstrating uniformity and symmetry in the connection relationship.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Plastic and non-GHG emissions are jointly regarded as key pollution management indicators.

Business Implications:
- Environmental performance assessment must simultaneously cover solid and air emission dimensions.
- Plastic packaging attributes directly affect resource circulation and disposal pressure.
- Segmentation by specific pollutant type facilitates the setting of differentiated management strategies.

Risk Focus:
- If the proportion of recyclable/compostable plastic is below 50%, disposal and compliance costs may rise significantly.
- If the tonnage of a single air pollutant type is significantly higher than other types, it may trigger limit constraints and regulatory penalties for that type of emission.
