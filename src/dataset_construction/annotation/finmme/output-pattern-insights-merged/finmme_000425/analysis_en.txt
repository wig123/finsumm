[Pattern Characteristics]
- The proportion of Bank consistently remains the largest segment across all years, but has continuously shrunk since FY21. The proportions of NBFC-MFI and SFBs have gradually expanded during the same period. NBFCs have slightly increased, while Others remain minimal and largely stable.
- The overall structure, from FY20 to 1Q24, has shifted from relative concentration around a single dominant entity to a landscape where the proportions of multiple institution types are relatively close. No sudden shifts occurred, only the Bank segment showing a noticeable, sustained decline after FY21.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The value share of credit originations is dispersing from traditional entities toward diversified institutions.

Business Implications:
- The dominance of leading institutions is weakening, resulting in a more fragmented landscape.
- The importance of NBFC-MFI and SFBs is rising, influencing the focus of resource allocation.
- The coexistence of multiple institutions increases demands for operational and collaborative management across different entities.

Risk Concerns:
- If the Bank proportion continues to fall below 30%, it could weaken overall stability and bargaining power.
- If the NBFC-MFI proportion remains above 40% long-term, concentration in a single customer segment or model could amplify volatility shocks.
