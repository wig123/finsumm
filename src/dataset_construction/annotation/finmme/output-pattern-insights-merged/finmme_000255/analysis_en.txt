[Pattern Characteristics]
The BNPP curve first dropped significantly, then reversed upward, showing a large overall amplitude, forming a "V" shape and continuing to extend upward during the forecast period. The S&P curve rose after reaching a historical low, intersecting with BNPP, and subsequently remained within a narrow range of variation. After the two curves converged in 2023, they began to gradually diverge. The BNPP forecast value is consistently higher than S&P, with the spread steadily widening and no clear inflection point.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: BNPP is significantly more optimistic about the medium-to-long-term production outlook than S&P.

Business Implications:
- BNPP anticipates greater capacity utilization and sales potential.
- S&P reflects a more restrained pace of industry expansion.
- The divergence between institutions is concentrated in the incremental growth assessment post-2024E.

Risk Concerns:
- If actual production falls below the BNPP 2025E forecast by approximately 1.6m units, this may indicate overly optimistic earnings and valuation assumptions.
- If industry production remains close to the S&P 16.8–17.0m range long-term, capital expenditures or inventory configured according to the BNPP scenario may result in over-investment.
