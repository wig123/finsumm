[Chart Components]
- Type: Multiple Line Chart
- Title: Transport is the biggest source of emissions in the EU
- Subtitle: Greenhouse gas emissions by sector (MtCO2e)
- X-axis: Year, approx. 1990—2025 (labeled 1990, 1995, 2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020, 2025)
- Y-axis: Emissions (MtCO2e), Range 0—1500 (Major ticks 500, 1000, 1500)
- Series/Legend: 6 lines—Transport (blue); Industry, Public Electricity & Heat, Buildings, Agriculture, Waste (gray, labeled on the right)
- Notes/Source: UNFCCC, EEA, Stratas Advisors; Transport includes international aviation and shipping

[Data Relationships]
- Transport: approx. 800 in 1990; peak approx. 1150 in 2007; approx. 900 in 2020; approx. 1050 in 2024.
- 2024 Sectors: Industry approx. 750–800; Public Electricity & Heat approx. 600–650; Buildings approx. 480–500; Agriculture approx. 360–380; Waste approx. 90–110.
- 2024 Comparison: Transport is higher than Industry by approx. 250–300; higher than Public Electricity & Heat by approx. 400–450; higher than Buildings by approx. 550; higher than Agriculture by approx. 680–700; higher than Waste by approx. 940–960.
- Transport Change (Start to End of Period): approx. +250 MtCO2e.

[Pattern Characteristics]
- Among all sectors, only the Transport line shows a net increase from the beginning to the end of the period; the remaining sectors generally show a gradual contraction or a stable slight decline.
- Transport formed its first local high point around 2007, subsequently declined, experienced a brief deep dip around 2020, and then rose again, maintaining a single dominant structure above all other lines in the latest year.
- The Industry and Public Electricity & Heat lines were close to each other at early high levels, then continuously declined and were surpassed by Transport; Buildings and Agriculture maintained narrow fluctuations at medium-low levels, while Waste showed a continuous monotonic decline with no significant rebound.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Emission reduction effectiveness highly depends on non-transport sectors, with Transport becoming the dominant source of emissions.

Business Implications:
- The overall emission reduction structure shows significant differences in contribution among sectors.
- The degree of Transport's impact on total emission levels is significantly higher than other sectors.
- If the current structure is maintained, the comprehensive emission reduction potential will increasingly concentrate on Transport.

Risk Focus:
- If Transport remains above 1000 MtCO2e for a long period, the decline in total emissions may be significantly constrained.
- If Industry and Public Electricity & Heat struggle to decline further after reaching around 500 MtCO2e, marginal emission reduction will rely more on Transport adjustments.
