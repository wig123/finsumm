[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Credit card delinquency levels are significantly higher than other loans, indicating clear structural differentiation.

Business Implications:
- The overall delinquency landscape is relatively stable, with no severe deterioration observed.
- Credit card assets have the greatest impact on overall risk exposure.
- Moderate attention should be paid to the upward pressure on delinquency for Personal loans and Consumer durables loans.

Risk Focus:
- Credit cards remaining above 10% may significantly increase portfolio provisions and losses.
- If Personal loans and Consumer durables loans exceed 5%, this will elevate the overall non-performing level of the retail portfolio.
