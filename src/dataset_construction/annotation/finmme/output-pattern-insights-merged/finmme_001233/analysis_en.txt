[Pattern Characteristics]
Attributes show a high-to-low gradient distribution in overall positive responses, with Hot water heating, Safety, and Reliability at the top, and Maintenance costs, Energy efficiency, and Running costs at the bottom. Attributes in the high group have a significantly larger proportion of “Very satisfied,” with fewer negative responses and neutral options, while cost and energy efficiency related attributes have a higher proportion of negative responses and “Don't know/Not applicable.” Space heating, highlighted by the black frame, is in the middle tier, with its positive and negative responses being at a moderate level among all attributes.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Functional experience outweighs cost and energy efficiency perception.

Business Implications:
- Performance of usage functions contributes more significantly to the overall evaluation.
- Cost and energy efficiency related sentiments drag down overall reputation.
- Space heating still has room for further optimization and promotion.

Risk Concerns:
- If dissatisfaction with running costs remains at ≈30%, it may inhibit subsequent purchases or recommendations.
- If the sum of dissatisfaction and uncertainty regarding energy efficiency exceeds ≈50%, it may weaken the appeal of energy-saving selling points.
