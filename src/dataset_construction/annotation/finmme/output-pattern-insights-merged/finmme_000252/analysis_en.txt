[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Inventory scale and turnover efficiency rise concurrently, leading to a significant increase in inventory capital commitment.

Business Implications:
- Implication 1 Current reliance on inventory has increased, resulting in a larger proportion of stock.
- Implication 2 Turnover efficiency has declined, with working capital increasingly locked up in inventory.
- Implication 3 The later structure is more concentrated in a high-inventory, high-DOI state, reducing flexibility.

Risk Focus:
- Risk Point 1 If inventory consistently exceeds 4,000 TWD mn, capital commitment pressure may significantly squeeze other expenditures.
- Risk Point 2 If DOI remains above 200 days for an extended period, it may lead to significant increases in inventory impairment or disposal costs.
