[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Both indicators experienced a boom followed by a retreat, and are currently at relatively weak levels.

Business Implications:
- The 2017–2019 phase reflects synchronously strong business and manufacturing sector prosperity.
- The sharp decline in 2020 indicates high vulnerability to shocks.
- The synchronous decline after 2021 suggests that the expansion of prosperity is unsustainable.

Risk Focus:
- If NFIB falls below 90, it may correspond to a significant deterioration in business expectations, leading to a contraction in investment and hiring plans.
- If ISM consistently remains below 45, it may indicate an intensification of manufacturing activity contraction, resulting in reduced employment and orders.
