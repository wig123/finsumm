- The latter part of the data slowly retreats from the local high point and concludes within the upper-middle range; the overall structure is continuous with no extreme outliers.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The capital adequacy level generally rose and then entered a plateau phase, with a slight recent decline.

Business Implications:
- Indicates that the capital buffer is more ample than in the initial period.
- The capacity to withstand asset volatility and absorb losses has strengthened.
- However, marginal improvement has been limited in the past two years, increasing the likelihood of tightening constraints.

Risk Focus:
- If the CET1 Capital Adequacy Ratio falls below 12%, the loss absorption capacity will be significantly compressed.
- If it remains consistently in the 12%–12.5% range, future expansion and regulatory flexibility may be constrained.
