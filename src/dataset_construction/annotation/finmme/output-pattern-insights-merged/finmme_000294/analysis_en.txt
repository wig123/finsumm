[Pattern Characteristics]
Both curves exhibit significant wave-like fluctuations, with synchronous high and low points occurring at multiple time intervals. PHEV generally remains in a higher range, with the gap between it and BEV gradually narrowing in the later period, briefly converging in the middle-to-late phase. At the end of the period, PHEV rapidly surged, while BEV, after retreating in the previous phase, rebounded but failed to reach a new high.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: PHEV proportion maintains dominance, but BEV share shows significant catch-up.

Business Implications:
- The increase in BEV share indicates a structural shift favoring pure electric products.
- PHEV remains the primary source of contribution, indicating high reliance on this sales structure.
- When the proportions of both are close, the powertrain configuration offers greater flexibility.

Risk Focus:
- If the BEV proportion falls back below 10%, the pace of return on investment for pure electric products may face pressure.
- If the PHEV proportion consistently remains above 25%, it may slow down the efficiency of transitioning to higher forms of electrification.
