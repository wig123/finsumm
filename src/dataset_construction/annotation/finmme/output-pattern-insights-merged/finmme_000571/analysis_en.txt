[Pattern Characteristics]
Overall, the actual values show a stepped upward trend from 2007–2020, followed by a noticeable decline after 2020, and then a gradual recovery. Forecasts from different quarterly versions form a fan-shaped band from low to high for the same year. Earlier versions are generally lower than later versions, with the largest divergence occurring in 2029. The period around 2020 is the main inflection point for the actual series. Long-term forecasts show a structure of annual increases and widening divergence after 2025.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The latest forecast significantly raises the long-term expectation for new wind power capacity addition.

Business Implications:
- Mid-to-long-term installation expectations are revised upwards, and growth potential is being reassessed.
- Project pipeline and capacity planning must align with higher levels of capacity addition.
- Uncertainty in long-term estimates is amplified; planning needs to emphasize flexible ranges.

Risk Focus:
- If the actual capacity in 2025 falls below 90 GW, it will be lower than the early high scenario, impacting mid-term return on investment expectations.
- If the actual capacity in 2029 falls below 135 GW, the deviation from the latest 170 GW expectation will be too large, potentially leading to upstream overcapacity and asset idling.
