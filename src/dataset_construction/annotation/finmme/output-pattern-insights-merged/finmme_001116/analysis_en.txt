[Pattern Characteristics]
Overall, all series showed a synchronized decline from 2021 to the end of 2022, after which the Total and the two consumption-related loan categories diverged. Personal business loans remained in the highest range throughout the period, exhibiting relatively small volatility. Non-mortgage consumer loans showed a unilateral increase after hitting a local low point at the end of 2022, while Mortgage loans broke below the zero axis around the same time and continued to hover near or below zero. The Total series, meanwhile, fluctuated narrowly at a low-to-moderate level.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Household credit growth has shifted from mortgages to non-mortgage consumption and commercial business operations.

Business Implications:
- Housing-related demand has weakened, and the focus of the loan structure has changed.
- The aggregate volume relies more heavily on business operation and consumer credit support.
- Asset portfolios show increased sensitivity to different loan types.

Risk Concerns:
- Mortgage growth persistently below 0% may drag down asset quality and Net Interest Margin (NIM) performance.
- Non-mortgage consumer loan growth consistently above 15% may lead to increased concentration risk and default exposure.
