Overall, emissions across various sectors exhibit divergent trends between 1990 and 2025, with most series shifting from high levels toward lower levels, while only one series gradually rises from a median level. The Transport series showed a slow increase around 2000‚ÄĒ2010, forming a clear peak, followed by a brief, sharp drop around 2020 before recovering again. [Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The emission structure shifts from being dominated by electricity to having a higher relative proportion contributed by transport.

Business Implications:
- Emission reduction effectiveness is concentrated in the electricity and industrial segments.
- Transport has become the critical weak link (or bottleneck area) in overall emission control.
- Buildings, Agriculture, and Waste contribute less but still possess room for continuous optimization.

Risk Concerns:
- If transport emissions persistently remain above approximately 1000 MtCO2e, a significant reduction in total emissions will be difficult to achieve.
- If Public Electricity & Heat emissions rebound above 1000 MtCO2e, existing emission reduction achievements will be undermined.
