[Pattern Characteristics]
- In all periods, Replacement occupies the largest proportion, followed by OEM, and Export is the smallest. The structure consistently exhibits a single dominant type.
- Replacement first contracted after FY21 and then slightly rebounded. OEM and Export simultaneously increased during the FY22–FY23 period. Subsequently, OEM remained stable, while Export slightly declined and stabilized.
- The proportional gap between Replacement and OEM generally narrowed before slightly widening, but it consistently remains at a high level, without any drastic structural shifts.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The revenue structure is slowly evolving from high dependence on Replacement towards a more balanced, diversified structure.

Business Implications:
- The proportion of OEM and Export is rising, leading to a more diversified customer structure.
- The Replacement-dominant structure remains unchanged, indicating continued reliance on a single segment.
- Structural adjustment has slowed down after FY23, making significant rebalancing unlikely in the short term.

Risk Focus:
- If the Replacement proportion exceeds 70% again, overall dependence on this channel will be excessively high.
- If Export remains below 10% for an extended period, the momentum and flexibility for external market expansion will be limited.
