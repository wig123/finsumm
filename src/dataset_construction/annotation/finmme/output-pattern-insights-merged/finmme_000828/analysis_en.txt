[Pattern Characteristics]
The overall direction of the two time series is generally consistent, with multiple instances where peaks and troughs occur close in time. Both were simultaneously at a clear low near 2009 and 2020, and simultaneously at a clear high near 2021–2022, after which the green line retracted more significantly than the orange line. Overall volatility is primarily characterized by phased, step-wise upward movement, interspersed with several rapid and substantial adjustments.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Valuation and profit margins are synchronized long-term, but valuation has recently contracted relative to profit margins.

Business Implications:
- Changes in profit margins remain an important pricing basis for valuation.
- Current valuation reaction to profit margins is more conservative compared to previous periods.
- If profit margins are maintained at current levels, valuation flexibility is constrained.

Risk Focus:
- If profit margins fall below 6%, historically this corresponds to a significant downward shift in valuation, potentially triggering further price adjustments.
- If valuation falls below 0.9 while profit margins remain around 9%, this may reflect amplified emotional pressure, which is detrimental to portfolio drawdown.
