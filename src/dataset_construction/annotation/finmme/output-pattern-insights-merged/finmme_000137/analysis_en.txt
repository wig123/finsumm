[Chart Components]
- Type: Line Chart
- Title: None
- Axes:
  - X-axis: Year range 2005 to 2025
  - Left Y-axis: Value range 4 to 8 (Applicable to wage growth and core services CPI)
  - Right Y-axis: Value range 1 to 3.5 (Applicable to Openings to unemployment rate ratio)
- Legend:
  - Green line: Atlanta Fed median wage growth (3mma), Left axis
  - Cyan line: US core services CPI (% y/y), Left axis
  - Dark blue solid line: Openings to unemployment rate (ratio), Right axis

[Data Relationships]
- Key Metrics and Changes:
  - Around 2020, wage growth and core services CPI rose significantly.
  - Since 2005, wage growth has generally been higher than core services CPI.
  - The Openings to unemployment rate ratio rose rapidly in 2020 and decreased slightly in 2022.

- Extremum Comparison:
  - Wage growth peaked in 2021, close to 8.
  - The Openings to unemployment rate ratio peaked in 2022, close to 3.5.

- Key Trends:
  - After 2010, wage growth and core services CPI rose synchronously.
  - The Openings to unemployment rate ratio shows an inverse trend compared to wage growth and CPI, especially noticeable after 2020.

[Pattern Characteristics]
- The two lines on the left axis are generally synchronous, with stronger correlation after 2010; and the green line remains above the cyan line most of the time.
- A significant jump occurred around 2020, forming a peak, after which both entered a downward phase.
- The dark blue line on the right axis rapidly rose in 2020 before falling back, showing an inverse direction relative to the two lines on the left axis, with a timing mismatch.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Core services CPI is primarily driven by wages; the job ratio serves as a control indicator.

Business Implications:
- Core services CPI is sensitive to wage movements.
- Decision-making prioritizes tracking wage indicators.
- The job ratio is used for cross-validation.

Risk Focus:
- Wage growth rate ≥ 8: CPI is often high, leading to potential judgment bias.
- Job ratio ≥ 3: Contradicts wage/CPI signals, leading to potential misinterpretation.
