[Pattern Characteristics]
The red bars generally fluctuate slowly within a relatively narrow range, slightly lower in the middle section and slightly higher at both ends, forming a distinct plateau around 2021–2022. The black line oscillates significantly above and below the zero axis, showing multiple sharp peaks and deep pullbacks in the early and mid-to-late stages. Its volatility is far greater than that of the red bars. The two series move in the same direction during certain periods, but around 2021, a clear divergence structure emerged where the red bars were at a high level while the black line was near zero or negative.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: There is a noticeable increase in periods where the pace of loan expansion is decoupled from the level of corporate buffer.

Business Implications:
- Loan growth rate and repayment buffer are not always synchronized.
- The pace of credit extension significantly impacts the stability of asset quality.
- Periods of high buffer coupled with low growth rates may indicate allocation redundancy.

Risk Focus:
- When Loan y-y is near or below 0%, if the red bars remain high, it may lead to a decrease in capital utilization efficiency.
- When Loan y-y exceeds 30% and the red bars are at the lower bound of the range, it may amplify interest rate and default losses.
