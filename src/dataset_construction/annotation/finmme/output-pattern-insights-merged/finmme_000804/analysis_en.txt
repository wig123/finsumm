[Pattern Characteristics]
- Vertically, NBFCs consistently hold the absolute majority share, while Private Banks occupy a relatively small segment.
- After FY20, the proportion of Private Banks gradually increased period by period, and NBFCs contracted accordingly. The gap was smallest in FY23, followed by a pullback in 1Q24 compared to FY23.
- The shares of both parties show a mirror image change across periods, with similar fluctuation ranges, indicating overall structural stability without sudden jumps.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: NBFCs maintain long-term dominance, while the share of Private Banks gradually increases but remains secondary.

Business Implications:
- The market structure dominated by NBFCs has not been fundamentally altered.
- Private Banks have enhanced their bargaining power in customer acquisition and loan origination value.
- The pullback in 1Q24 suggests uncertainty in the growth momentum of Private Banks.

Risk Focus:
- If the Private Banks' share falls back below 15%, the returns from private channel deployment may come under pressure.
- If the NBFCs' share returns above 85%, excessive market concentration may weaken negotiation power and product diversity.
