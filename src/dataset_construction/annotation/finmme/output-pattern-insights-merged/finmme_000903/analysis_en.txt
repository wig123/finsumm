[Pattern Characteristics]
- Price maintained a low, narrow range in the early period, followed by two phased, step-like increases, and subsequently retreated slightly after forming a global high plateau in the second phase.
- Prices within the "Recession" shaded areas are mostly at local highs or relatively high levels following an ascent, while non-shaded areas are typically early lows or adjustment ranges following a high.
- Distinct local and global peak ranges formed near 1973–1974 and 1979–1981, during which price volatility was relatively concentrated and the magnitude was far greater than in other years.

[Core Insights]

Core Conclusion: The center of gravity for crude oil prices shifted significantly upward and remained high during the sample period.

Business Implications:
- The energy cost structure experienced a significant elevation and repricing.
- The sensitivity of profitability in related industries to oil prices significantly increased.
- Periods of macroeconomic volatility exhibit synchronous characteristics with high oil price ranges.

Risk Focus:
- When prices approach 40 $/BBL, cost-side pressure may significantly compress profit margins.
- Prices sustained above 30 $/BBL for several years may induce demand contraction and operational volatility.
