- Across the four charts, the curves for various holdings and weightings generally show a unidirectional change from high to low, stabilizing near zero in the middle-to-later periods and remaining relatively flat.
- There are significant differences in proportion among different investor types, with local versus foreign, and institutional versus retail, showing a structure where one party is numerically dominant over the long term.
- Localized step-like changes occurred around 2019 and 2020, after which the curves returned to a state of slow adjustment. All curves are currently in historical low ranges at the end of the period.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: ROTI is marginally allocated in both major investor portfolios and the company's equity structure.

Business Implications:
- Limited presence in institutional holdings and indices, resulting in weak influence.
- Trading and liquidity rely more heavily on a small number of long-term funds.
- Retail and passive funds have limited capacity to support the price.

Risk Focus:
- If the JCI weighting continues to hover around 0.02%, index funds may contract further, amplifying the impact of single transaction shocks.
- If retail shareholding remains below 1% for an extended period, insufficient buying support during price volatility may lead to a widening of short-term price spreads.
