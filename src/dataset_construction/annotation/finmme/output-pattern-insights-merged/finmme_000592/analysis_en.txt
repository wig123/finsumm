[Pattern Characteristics]
- The two series coincide at the starting point, then generally exhibit a diverging structure where one curve is significantly higher and the other is relatively lower, with the gap widening over time.
- The dark series maintains a unidirectional expansion throughout the entire period, accompanied by minor plateaus and localized pullbacks; the light series experiences a phased decline in the early to middle period, followed by a gradual rise in the later period, but consistently remains below the dark series.
- Approximately around the 1970s, the two lines transitioned from being close to clearly diverging, after which the gap continued to expand, and a high-level inflection point leading to a pullback appeared around 2019–2021.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: A long-term structural divergence has emerged between output efficiency and average wage performance.

Business Implications:
- The feedback ratio of labor compensation to efficiency gains continues to decline.
- Income distribution relies more heavily on contributions from non-wage factors.
- The sensitivity of labor returns to macroeconomic output expansion has weakened.

Risk Focus:
- When the gap between the two exceeds 150 percentage points, labor incentives may be significantly weakened.
- If the growth rate of average wages remains below 100% for an extended period and the gap continues to widen, it may suppress performance in consumption-related businesses.
