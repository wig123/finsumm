[Pattern Characteristics]
In the early stages, the two series were largely synchronous, with similar upward momentum. The blue line was generally slightly higher and exhibited greater volatility. The red line showed a single, distinct trough in mid-2020. Subsequently, the red line continued a sustained, unilateral ascent and has remained above the blue line long-term since 2021, with the gap between the two gradually widening. The blue line formed a cyclical peak near the end of 2021, followed by a significant decline. It recovered later but failed to surpass the previous high, establishing a persistent divergence structure relative to the red line.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The sales intensity of automotive chips significantly exceeds that of the overall semiconductor market.

Business Implications:
- Automotive-related demand provides relatively stronger support.
- Revenue structure shows increasing reliance on the automotive sector.
- The slowdown in overall semiconductor market prosperity has a relatively limited impact on the automotive sector.

Risk Focus:
- If the red line falls below 2.0x, the relative advantage will narrow, potentially dragging down overall growth.
- If the blue line falls below 1.5x, it indicates overall industry pressure, increasing exposure to volatility in a single sector.
