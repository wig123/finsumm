[Core Insights]
- Core Conclusion: Net profit is more sensitive to Gross Margin, and secondarily sensitive to Overseas Sales.

Business Implications:
- Adjusting Gross Margin alone has a more pronounced impact on profitability levels.
- When sales and Gross Margin change simultaneously, profit elasticity is amplified.
- The current baseline is in a neutral zone, with clear profit/loss elasticity zones on both sides.

Risk Focus:
- When Gross Margin drops to -2pp and sales are -10%, the impact is -11.1%, which could significantly compress profit.
- When Gross Margin declines at any level and sales are ≤-5%, the impact is <-5.6%; cost reduction and pricing strategies need to be evaluated.
