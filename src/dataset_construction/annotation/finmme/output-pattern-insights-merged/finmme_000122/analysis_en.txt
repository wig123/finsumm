[Chart Components]
- Type: Line chart
- Title: Mobileye, NVDA & QCOM automotive chip revenue
- Axes:
  - X-axis: Time (Quarterly), from Mar-16 to Dec-23
  - Y-axis: Quarterly Revenue (Millions of USD), range from 0 to 650
- Legend:
  - Mobileye (Intel): Gray line and green diamond
  - NVDA: Black line and black triangle
  - QCOM: Blue line and blue square

[Data Relationships]
- Key Metrics and Changes:
  - Mobileye's revenue grew steadily starting in 2016, reaching a peak of approximately 650 million USD in 2023.
  - NVDA's revenue was relatively stable, starting at approximately 100 million USD and growing to about 250 million USD in 2023.
  - QCOM rose rapidly after 2021, reaching approximately 600 million USD in 2023.

- Extremum Comparison:
  - Mobileye's revenue was the highest in 2023, exceeding 650 million USD, higher than both QCOM and NVDA.
  - NVDA's revenue consistently remained the lowest.

- Significant Trends:
  - Mobileye and QCOM showed significant growth trends throughout the period, especially in the later stages.
  - NVDA's growth was relatively slow and stable.

[Pattern Characteristics]
The overall structure is a continuous time series. In the later period, the hierarchical differences among the three series are clear, presenting a diversified and dispersed structure. Mobileye and QCOM experienced steep acceleration in the latter half and remained in a higher range, while NVDA maintained a narrower platform. Inflection points exist: Mobileye briefly declined in 2020, QCOM saw a step-change leap starting in 2021, and NVDA experienced a single surge followed by a decline in 2022.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: Automotive chip revenue forms a duopoly structure (two strong players), with NVDA ranking last.

Business Implications:
- Revenue concentration increased toward Mobileye and QCOM
- NVDA's automotive segment contribution is relatively low
- Potential for changes in the leading position

Risk Concerns:
- QCOM quarterly revenue dropping below 450mm, making the leading position vulnerable
- NVDA remaining below 200mm long-term, making ranking improvement difficult
