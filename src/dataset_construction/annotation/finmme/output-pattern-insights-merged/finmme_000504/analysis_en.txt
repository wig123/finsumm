Core Conclusion: The LDR maintained overall stability during the sample period, with significant contraction only occurring during specific phases.

Business Implications:
- In the long term, the matching degree between credit extension and deposit sources is relatively stable.
- The matching degree decreased in 2020–2021, potentially impacting asset return contribution.
- The matching degree has recovered since 2022, but the utilization level relative to the high end is lower than that of 2015–2019.

Risk Concerns:
- If the LDR approaches 98 again, the over-utilization of funds may compress liquidity buffers.
- If the LDR falls below 88, it may lead to idle deposits and drag down asset return levels.
