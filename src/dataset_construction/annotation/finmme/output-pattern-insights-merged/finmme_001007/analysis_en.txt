[Pattern Characteristics]

- The black line exhibits several distinct high-low cyclical intervals: After oscillating at a high level in the early stage, a round of deep weakening occurred around 2010, followed by a recovery to a medium-high level; after 2014, it gradually declined, forming the lowest point of the entire sample in 2021, and subsequently recovered from the low point to a moderate level.
- The two series show different combinations of inverse and synchronous movements in certain phases: For example, during 2020—2021, when the bar maintained a relatively high level, the line was in the bottom range of the entire period; whereas during the early high-growth phase, the bar was mostly at the sample's moderate or lower level.

[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: There is a phased mismatch and rebalancing between loan growth rate and corporate coverage level.

Business Implications:
- Early loan expansion relied more on external demand rather than coverage level support.
- The rise in coverage during 2020—2021 did not synchronously drive credit expansion.
- The current combination is in a moderate range, with limited room for maneuver.

Risk Focus:
- If coverage drops below 1.5 and loan growth remains below 5%, it may lead to asset returns insufficient to cover costs.
- If loan growth rises again above 25% while coverage remains around 2.0, it may increase the concentration of credit exposure.
