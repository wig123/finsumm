[Core Insights]
Core Conclusion: The difference between Seasonally Adjusted (SA) and Non-Seasonally Adjusted (NSA) Core CPI shows clear periodic fluctuations.

Business Implications:
- Seasonal adjustment methods introduce deviations in opposite directions during different phases.
- Inflation assessment, if based solely on a single metric, is prone to judgment errors.
- The range of high and low differences can be used to identify periods of strong or weak seasonal factors.

Risk Focus:
- When the difference is below -2.0, ignoring the adjustment method may lead to an overestimation of the degree of inflation deceleration.
- When the difference is above +3.0, directly extrapolating the trend may lead to a misjudgment of inflation persistence.
